Regeneration & Gentrification
I was born in London and continued to live in the city for the next 24 years of my life, over which time I saw the complete regeneration of Chinatown, Camden Town, where I spent a lot of time as a teenager, and Elephant & Castle, where I spent a lot of my adult life living in close proximity to.

I then moved to Norwich, when consultations for the regeneration of Anglia Square were taking place. I did an investigation into this process during my MA, during the Forensic Architecture Studio. Trying to find a permanent venue and speaking to property developers for Even also showed the unavoidable relation between the arts and regeneration.

I take an interest in the relationship between existing communities and proposed redevelopment schemes, considering what kind of approach leads to preventing displacement. I am particularly interested in the relationship between the arts and regeneration, the provision of meanwhile spaces as a method of displacement and gentrification.

Network Infrastructure, Protest & Digital Communication
I was on Tumblr a lot as a teenager, during the riot grrl revival and went to a lot of meet ups. This was peak social media for me. I did a module in Film & New Media which introduced me to a lot of critical writing on the internet and social media.

Since then, I've taken a lot of interest in the possibilities for the internet as a global communication network beyond monopolised platforms. I use a lot of open-source software, self-teach myself to do code through small projects (like this website) and take a general interest in the opaque nature of user-friendly, "smooth" UIs, and the infrastructures that deliver the internet to us.

As a facilitator, I think a lot about how we communicate and the impacts of networks on our communicative capacity, and I pay attention to the omnipresence of the digital in the social.
